# OSU Strategic Plan — Strategic Alignment options

This file holds OSU's strategic plan (*Prosperity Widely Shared*) so the skill can
offer authors a **pick-list** for the brief's **Strategic Alignment** section,
instead of asking them to recall a goal from memory. Naming a *specific* plan
commitment is exactly what reviewers look for (see `rubric.md` → Strategic
Alignment), so this turns a commonly-weak section into a quick, accurate choice.

## How the skill uses this file

When working the Strategic Alignment section (Step 3 interview, brief mode):

1. Read the goal/commitment list under **Strategic alignment options** below.
2. Present it to the author as a **numbered pick-list** in a fenced `questions`
   block — one goal per line — and let them answer by number (one or more), e.g.
   "Which of OSU's strategic goals does this most directly advance? (pick any)".
3. Take the author's pick(s) verbatim as the named commitment(s), then ask them
   (in their own words) *how* the work advances it — do **not** invent the
   connection yourself. Write the result into `strategic_alignment` as the named
   goal + the author's one-line rationale.
4. If nothing fits, that's a real signal — capture it plainly rather than forcing
   a goal, and note it for the readiness summary.

Never assert an alignment the author didn't choose, and never reword a goal into
something the plan doesn't say (this is a *facts come from the source* rule, same
as the two hard rules in `SKILL.md`). If this file still only contains the
placeholder below, fall back to the open-ended Strategic alignment question in
`reviewer-questions.md` and tell the author the pick-list isn't loaded yet.

## Strategic alignment options

*Prosperity Widely Shared* (OSU's strategic plan, horizon 2030) is organized as
**three goals** (the "whats"), advanced by **five actions** (the "hows"), with
**five top targets** (the measures). Present the **three goals** as the pick-list
first — three options fits the chat's question panel — then, once the author has
picked, sharpen the alignment with the matching action or target below.

### The three goals (pick-list — present these)

When you put these in a `questions` block, use **only the bolded one-line goal
titles — exactly one line per goal** (the chat panel renders one pick per line
and shows at most five lines). The indented text is context for *you*, to help
map the author's idea to a goal — don't paste it into the pick-list.

1. **A university focused on big discoveries that drive big solutions.**
   - Context: research fully powered up for excellence, concentrated where OSU is
     uniquely competitive — climate science and related solutions, clean energy
     technology and related solutions, robotics, integrated health and biotechnology.
2. **A university where every student graduates.**
   - Context: every admitted student — in person or online — supported to achieve
     their goal to graduate, without compromising academic standards.
3. **A university that fuels a thriving world.**
   - Context: a proactive driver of economic, workforce and community development,
     translating research into applications with partners in industry, government
     and education.

### The five actions (use to sharpen a picked goal)

1. **Launch a campaign for timely undergraduate degree completion** — retention
   and graduation targets in every unit, more need-based scholarships, a signature
   first-year onboarding experience, stronger community-college pathways.
2. **Build international research distinction in areas of competitive advantage**
   — invest in the four priority research areas, and build **foundational strength
   in artificial intelligence, data science and research computing across Oregon
   State** (often the most direct hook for UIT technology proposals).
3. **Build an enterprise approach to knowledge translation, innovation and
   partnerships** — economic development and industry relations strategy, research
   translation capacity, a Portland metro strategy, global networks.
4. **Build faculty excellence** — targeted hiring, professional development in
   course design/teaching/assessment, recognition for distinguished teaching.
5. **Significantly increase enrollment online and at OSU-Cascades** — more than
   double online-only enrollment; growth strategies for OSU-Cascades, the
   Corvallis campus and professional graduate programs.

### Top five targets by 2030 (cite one when the work moves a number)

1. Increase annual research expenditures to $600 million.
2. Increase the six-year graduation rate to 80 percent.
3. Equalize six-year graduation rates (by race and ethnicity).
4. Increase online-only enrollment to 30,000.
5. Increase OSU-Cascades enrollment to 2,200.

Also relevant for infrastructure/operations proposals: the plan's **Work
Underway** list continues efforts such as the Jen-Hsun and Lori Huang
Collaborative Innovation Complex, accelerated facilities renewal, and the
**Administrative Modernization Program (AMP)** — a proposal supporting these may
cite the plan's implementation commitments rather than forcing a goal.
